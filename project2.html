two Akasas

Two Akasas is a multimedia self-portrait about living between multiple versions of the self. The project reflects how my identity is shaped through names, culture, language, gender expression, and the different expectations I move through in different spaces. Rather than presenting identity as fixed or singular, the work imagines it as layered, unstable, and constantly shifting.

The title refers to the coexistence of two parallel selves: one that is socially readable and outwardly performed, and another that feels more private, fluid, and internally lived. Through duplication, distortion, and glitch, the project shows how these versions do not exist separately, but overlap, clash, and continuously remake one another. The work becomes a portrait of identity as contradiction, negotiation, and ongoing construction.

process

This project was created through multiple media processes. The image of me in costume was generated with AI using Gemini, while the background, title text, and glitch effects were coded by me in p5.js. I then edited these elements together in CapCut as a short video, adding motion and visual effects to shape the final piece.
